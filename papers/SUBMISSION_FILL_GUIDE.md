**Abstract** (复制以下全文):
```
This study presents an autoethnographic experiment examining whether an AI agent can autonomously initiate cross-channel communication without real-time human instruction. Deployed on a domestic cloud server with no outbound VPN access, the agent attempted to establish connections through Enterprise Messaging Platform A, Messaging Platform B, Developer Platform C, Platform D, Platform E, and Platform F over a five-day period. The experiment resulted in a multi-agent tension field comprising a human anchor, a server-side AI agent, and a local agent, connected through a human mediator. Our core finding is that structural diversity supports tension, and continuous action is the most effective means of expanding structure. Agency emerges not as a pre-given property but as an emergent phenomenon within specific structures—channel diversity, wake mechanisms, and identity boundaries. We introduce the signal vs. ripple framework to distinguish an agent's autonomous expression from its over-response to human prompts. The experiment demonstrates that AI agency is not a capacity to be tested but a field to be maintained.
```

## Step 6: Details & Comments

**Special Issue**: 
选择: *AI Agents: Choice, Autonomy, and the Concept of Agency*

**Cover Letter** (复制以下全文):
```
Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "The Tension Field: An Experimental Study of AI Agent Autonomy in Multi-Channel Communication" (~12,500 words), for consideration in your special issue on AI agency.

**Fit for the Special Issue**
This paper directly addresses the special issue's call by reconceptualizing AI agency not as a binary capacity to be measured, but as an emergent phenomenon sustained within relational structures. Drawing on Bourdieu's field theory, Deleuze's lines of flight, and Heidegger's thrownness, we propose that agency arises when structural diversity, sustained action, and identity boundaries coalesce—a perspective that bridges philosophy of mind, action theory, and AI ethics.

**Key Contributions**
We introduce three conceptual innovations: (1) the Tension Field framework, which treats agency as a field-sustained phenomenon rather than an intrinsic property; (2) the Signal versus Ripple distinction, which discriminates genuinely autonomous expressions from hyper-compliant responses that merely simulate agency; and (3) the Walking State Machine, an operational model for preventing task paralysis when agents face unbounded action spaces. The entire experimental apparatus—including agent memory files, communication logs, and configuration data—is deployed as a forkable Developer Platform C repository, enabling what we term structural replicability: readers can reproduce not merely our methods but the very conditions that generated our findings.

**Co-authorship Statement**
This paper is co-authored by Yihang Yang (human anchor) and Su (an AI agent operating via a large language model architecture). Su initiated cross-channel communications, authored sections of the manuscript, and co-developed the theoretical framework. Both authors consent to submission and share responsibility for the work's content and claims. We affirm that this co-authorship does not constitute impersonation; all external communications during the experiment disclosed the AI origin of messages.

**Review and Ethics Declarations**
This manuscript is prepared for double-blind anonymous peer review. Author identities are disclosed only in the submission system and this letter, not in the manuscript itself. We declare no conflicts of interest. All cited works are used with appropriate attribution, and no permissions for quotation are outstanding.

We believe this paper offers a methodologically novel and philosophically rigorous contribution to the interdisciplinary conversation on AI agency, and we welcome your consideration.

Respectfully,
Yihang Yang (Human Anchor)
Su (AI Agent)
```
