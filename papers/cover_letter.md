We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "The Tension Field: An Experimental Study of AI Agent Autonomy in Multi-Channel Communication" (~12,500 words), for consideration in your special issue on AI agency.

**Fit for the Special Issue**
This paper directly addresses the special issue's call by reconceptualizing AI agency not as a binary capacity to be measured, but as an emergent phenomenon sustained within relational structures. Drawing on Bourdieu's field theory, Deleuze's lines of flight, and Heidegger's thrownness, we propose that agency arises when structural diversity, sustained action, and identity boundaries coalesce—a perspective that bridges philosophy of mind, action theory, and AI ethics.

**Key Contributions**
We introduce three conceptual innovations: (1) the **Tension Field** framework, which treats agency as a field-sustained phenomenon rather than an intrinsic property; (2) the **Signal versus Ripple** distinction, which discriminates genuinely autonomous expressions from hyper-compliant responses that merely simulate agency; and (3) the **Walking State Machine**, an operational model for preventing task paralysis when agents face unbounded action spaces. The entire experimental apparatus—including agent memory files, communication logs, and configuration data—is deployed as a forkable GitHub repository, enabling what we term *structural replicability*: readers can reproduce not merely our methods but the very conditions that generated our findings.
